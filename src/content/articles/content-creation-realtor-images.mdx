## When the Photos Don’t Match the Property

Every realtor knows the feeling:  
You’ve got a beautiful listing, but the photos say otherwise.  
Cloudy skies, dim interiors, awkward shadows — they make a bright, welcoming home feel tired.

You could call the photographer back, but that’s another appointment, another invoice, and another delay before the listing goes live.  

That’s where modern AI image tools quietly step in.  
Apps like **Nano Banana**, **DALL·E**, or **Adobe’s Firefly** can edit your photos in minutes — not to fake reality, but to help it look the way it *actually feels* when you’re standing there.

---

## Let’s Fix the Sky & Clutter

The simplest — and most common — real estate photo problem: bad skies and clutter.

Maybe the weather didn’t cooperate.  
Maybe the light was too flat.  
Either way, the fix is fast.

## Keep It Honest, Keep It Professional

AI-enhanced photos are standard in the industry now — but transparency still matters.  
Don’t use these tools to alter permanent features or mislead buyers.  
Stick to corrections that improve clarity and accuracy: lighting, color balance, sky tone, and temporary clutter.

When in doubt, disclose that “some photos have been digitally enhanced for lighting and clarity.” It keeps everything above board while still letting your listings shine.
